Our Industrial & Fleet Washing Process

Thorough, Efficient, and Safe for Your Equipment

  1. Assessment & Pre-Treatment

    We evaluate the equipment type, soil level, and any sensitive components before applying the right degreaser or pre-soak.

  2. High-Powered Degreasing

    Commercial degreasers break down oil, grease, diesel soot, and road grime that can't be shifted by water pressure alone.

  3. Pressure Washing

    High-temperature, high-pressure washing blasts away loosened buildup across all surfaces, undercarriage, and tight areas.

  4. Final Rinse & Inspection

    A thorough rinse followed by an inspection ensures everything is clean and no equipment is left worse than we found it.

Industrial & Fleet Washing FAQ

Do you come to our facility or yard?

Yes. On-site washing is our standard approach for fleet and equipment work — we bring everything needed to your location so your vehicles and equipment don't lose service time traveling to a wash bay.

How often should commercial fleets be washed?

It depends on the operation. Local delivery fleets often benefit from weekly or bi-weekly washing. Construction equipment and heavy machinery typically needs cleaning monthly or after major project phases. We'll recommend a schedule based on your specific operation.

Can you clean equipment with electrical or sensitive components?

Yes. We're experienced working around sensitive areas and adjust pressure, direction, and distance accordingly. We communicate with your operators about any specific components before starting.

Do you handle wastewater containment for industrial washing?

We can discuss wastewater management requirements with you during the quoting process to ensure we meet any applicable local environmental requirements for your site.

Do you offer scheduled fleet washing programs?

Yes. We offer ongoing fleet washing programs — weekly, bi-weekly, monthly, or seasonal — with consistent scheduling so your vehicles are always clean and you're never calling around for service.

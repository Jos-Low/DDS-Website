Solar Panel Cleaning FAQ

How much efficiency do dirty solar panels lose?

Studies show dirty panels can lose 15–25% of their energy output, and in high-pollen or dusty environments even more. In Michigan, spring pollen season is one of the biggest culprits — panels can lose significant output within weeks if not cleaned.

How often should solar panels be cleaned in Michigan?

Twice a year is the standard recommendation — once in spring after pollen season and once in fall before winter. More frequent cleaning may benefit panels near roads, farms, or areas with heavy bird activity.

Won't rain clean my solar panels?

Rain removes some loose dust, but it can't break down bird droppings, pollen film, or mineral deposits from hard water. These require proper cleaning to fully remove.

Is your cleaning method safe for my panel warranty?

Yes. We use non-abrasive tools and purified water consistent with manufacturer recommendations. We never use pressure washing directly on panel glass.

Do you clean commercial solar arrays?

Yes. We service large commercial and industrial ground-mount systems across Michigan. Contact us for a custom quote on larger installations.
